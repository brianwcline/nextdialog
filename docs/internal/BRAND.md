## Brand Name

**NextDialog**

One word. Capital N, capital D. Never "Next Dialog," "next dialog," "nextDialog," or "ND."

The name carries two meanings:
- **Next** — what comes after the terminal tab era. The next step in how developers work with AI agents.
- **Dialog** — a conversation, not a command. The relationship between human and agent is collaborative, not hierarchical.

Together: the next conversation between you and your work.

## Positioning

### The One-Sentence Position

NextDialog is the only AI agent session manager designed for how humans feel, not how computers perform.

### The Landscape

Every tool in this space — Claude Squad, opcode, Agent Deck, 1Code, Claude Code Desktop — solves the same problem: "I have too many terminal tabs." They compete on features: more panels, more config, more power, more information density.

NextDialog doesn't compete on features. It competes on feeling.

### Who We're For

Developers running multiple AI coding agents who are energized by what's possible but exhausted by the cognitive overhead of managing it all. They don't want fewer agents. They want to stop drowning in them.

### Who We're Not For

Power users who want maximum information density and total control. People who enjoy the cockpit. People who measure their day in PRs shipped. Those developers are well-served by existing tools. We're not trying to convert them.

### The Competitive Line

We are not a Claude Code wrapper. We are agent-agnostic. Claude Code, Codex, Aider, Gemini CLI, OpenClaw, plain terminals — NextDialog manages the human, not the agent.

## Brand Pillars

### 1. Calm Over Chaos

The default state is serene. The gradient breathes. The cards are still. Motion means something — it's never decorative. When everything is fine, the screen says nothing. When something needs you, it says exactly that and nothing more.

*This pillar governs:* visual design, animation choices, notification philosophy, information density.

### 2. Feeling Over Features

We will never win a feature comparison chart against opcode or 1Code. We don't want to. Our advantage is that people FEEL better using NextDialog. That feeling is the feature. Design decisions that sacrifice feeling for functionality are wrong, even if they're "objectively useful."

*This pillar governs:* product roadmap, feature requests (most get declined), marketing language, what we say no to.

### 3. Human Over Terminal

The app exists for the person at the desk, not for the processes in the terminal. We don't care what agent is running. We care whether the human knows what needs their attention, and whether they feel in control. The terminal is a means. The human is the end.

*This pillar governs:* agent-agnostic architecture, status detection design, the "return to calm" interaction, why we're light mode.

### 4. Presence Over Productivity

We don't track metrics. We don't count PRs. We don't gamify. We don't optimize throughput. We help developers be PRESENT with their work — aware of the whole picture, responsive when needed, at peace when not. Presence is the precondition for good work. We provide the precondition, not the measurement.

*This pillar governs:* what we never build (dashboards, analytics, streaks), how we talk about the product, what success looks like.

### 5. Twilight Over Terminal

A philosophical choice, not a mode toggle. Every developer tool defaults to dark-mode-terminal-energy — pure black, neon accents, information density. nextDialog rejects that, but it also rejects the cotton-candy pastel of consumer apps. We live in the **twilight** — deep, atmospheric, contemplative. Burgundy, teal, coral, warm cream. The quality of light at dusk, when warm and cool coexist. Like standing before a Turrell installation, not sitting at a terminal.

*This pillar governs:* color palette, atmospheric gradients, visual identity, why we look like nothing else in the developer tool ecosystem.

## Voice & Tone

### Who NextDialog Sounds Like

A thoughtful colleague who's calm under pressure. Not a brand. Not a startup. Not a hype machine. Someone who's been through the chaos of managing 10 terminal tabs and found a better way — and shares it without evangelizing.

### Voice Attributes

| Attribute | What it means | What it doesn't mean |
|---|---|---|
| **Calm** | Measured, unhurried, confident | Boring, passive, detached |
| **Direct** | Says what it means, no filler | Blunt, aggressive, dismissive |
| **Honest** | Acknowledges tradeoffs, doesn't oversell | Self-deprecating, uncertain |
| **Warm** | Genuinely cares about the human | Cheery, performative, "Hey there! 👋" |
| **Opinionated** | Has a point of view, states it clearly | Preachy, condescending, dismissive of alternatives |

### Tone by Context

**Website / Marketing:**
Confident and clear. Lead with the feeling, not the feature. Don't explain what the app does before explaining why it exists. Let the reader feel the problem before offering the solution.

> ✅ "You run 5 agents. You manage 15 tabs. You're losing your mind."
> ❌ "NextDialog is a powerful multi-agent session management tool built with Tauri 2."

**README / Documentation:**
Practical and respectful. Assume the reader is smart. Don't over-explain. Show, don't tell. Code examples over paragraphs.

> ✅ "NextDialog manages any terminal-based agent. Claude Code, Codex, Aider — bring what you use."
> ❌ "NextDialog is an innovative solution that leverages cutting-edge technology to provide developers with a seamless multi-agent orchestration experience."

**Social / Twitter:**
Human and specific. Share real moments, real screenshots, real feelings. Never hype. Never "excited to announce." Just show the work.

> ✅ "Closed a terminal, saw all my sessions at a glance, realized nothing needed me. That 2-second exhale is the whole product."
> ❌ "🚀 Excited to announce NextDialog v0.2! Packed with amazing new features! Check it out!"

**Error Messages / UI Copy:**
Short, honest, helpful. Never cute. Never blame the user. Always offer a next step.

> ✅ "Session stopped unexpectedly. Restart?"
> ❌ "Oops! Something went wrong 😅"

**Changelogs / Release Notes:**
State what changed and why. Connect changes to the philosophy when relevant. No feature-dumping.

> ✅ "Cards now show a hover preview of the last 3 terminal lines. You can decide whether a session needs you without opening it — less switching, more calm."
> ❌ "Added hover preview feature to session cards showing last 3 lines of terminal output."

## Core Message

### The 10-Second Version

NextDialog is a calm interface for managing AI coding agents. It works with all of them — Claude Code, Codex, Aider, anything. See every session at a glance. Click in only when needed. Return to calm when you're done.

### The 30-Second Version

Developers running multiple AI agents are drowning in terminal tabs. The work isn't hard — the overhead is. BCG research shows that 3+ concurrent AI tools cause a cognitive cliff: 33% more decision fatigue, 39% more errors. Not from the work. From the switching.

NextDialog is a desktop app that gives you one calm screen for all your agent sessions. Glass cards on a breathing gradient. Status dots that tell you what needs attention. Click a card to work. Press Escape to return to calm. It works with any terminal-based agent — Claude Code, Codex, Aider, or anything else you run.

Every other tool in this space competes on features. NextDialog competes on feeling.

### The Manifesto Version

Something broke when developers started running AI agents in parallel.

The agents got faster. The code got better. The developer got fried.

Not burned out — burning out is about volume. This is different. This is the cognitive tax of overseeing five minds at once. Checking if Claude is stuck. Wondering if Codex errored out. Switching between tabs so fast you forget which one you were looking at. The BCG research has a name for it: AI Brain Fry. 33% more decision fatigue. 39% more major errors. And it gets worse with every agent you add.

Every tool built to solve this problem made it worse. More panels. More features. More information. More density. Because the assumption was: the developer needs to see MORE.

The developer doesn't need to see more. The developer needs to see *enough* — and feel calm while doing it.

NextDialog is a quiet room in a loud building.

It shows you your agents as cards on a breathing gradient. A green dot means it's working. An amber dot means it needs you. You click in when you're needed. You press Escape when you're done. The gradient breathes. The cards are still. Nothing demands your attention until something deserves it.

It works with every agent you use. Claude Code. Codex. Aider. OpenClaw. Gemini. Plain terminals. It doesn't care what's running. It cares about the human watching the screen.

This is not a productivity tool. It's not an IDE. It's not a dashboard. It's the space between your terminals — the place you return to when you need to see the whole picture and remember that you're in control.

Every tool in this space optimizes for throughput.

NextDialog optimizes for you.
